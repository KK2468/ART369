Dwelling in the Never-Ending Present





The idea of the web, the internet, and the digital age seems to be so tied to the present, and could act as a potential gateway to the future. With the internet, we have the power to access all sorts of information with just the touch of our fingertips. This tool not only gives us the power to move from place to place and communicate with people from all over the world, but also the power to move across time. In this way, the internet gives us an opportunity to... transcend time & space
We can now “experience” the past through a digital medium--through… old photographs,

uploaded scans of documents... or artwork, videos, audio-tracks, blog-posts, archival collections (like the nytimes time machine), and so on. In this way, we are

uploading
our past, our collective past, onto the internet, and thus building an ever-growing archive of each of
        our stories.






Websites can be

        moved around,

            redirected,

                replaced,

                    and removed.

In this way, the web becomes much like history.
History in the sense that the stories of our past, be it on an individual-level, family-level, or national-level, can be altered and reshaped.

Given that, it appears that in life, as with the web, change is the only constant, and yet
plus ça change, plus c'est la même chose"
-Jean-Baptiste Alphonse Karr
So I suppose…

           The more things change the more they stay the same.

Now that we have found a way to save our past on the web and share it with select individuals or the greater public. But despite this digitalization, the idea of recorded history and how it is created, and our concept of time remains the same. So long as these sources are not deleted, like really deleted, we can go back down memory lane, understand history, or simply just to remember things that have happened
